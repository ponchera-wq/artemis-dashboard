The Axiom Extravehicular Mobility Unit (AxEMU) is a next-generation lunar spacesuit developed by Axiom Space, evolved from NASA's xEMU design. The AxEMU features improved mobility for kneeling and surface sample collection, a helmet with integrated HD camera and lights, 4G/LTE communications, and an 8-hour EVA duration. The suit can withstand the Moon's permanently shadowed south polar regions for up to 2 hours. Axiom completed its first uncrewed thermal vacuum test in November 2025 and is assembling the first flight unit in 2026.

SPACESUIT EVOLUTION

The AxEMU represents the next generation beyond NASA's Extravehicular Mobility Unit (EMU) used on the Space Shuttle and International Space Station since 1981. It also builds on NASA's xEMU design research conducted over the past decade. Axiom Space, selected by NASA in 2023, developed AxEMU specifically for lunar operations and long-duration surface exploration. The suit has been co-developed with input from astronauts and field geologists to optimize for lunar work.

KEY FEATURES

Improved Mobility

AxEMU's redesigned waist and hip joints allow astronauts to kneel, crouch, and bend — critical for collecting geological samples and traversing uneven terrain. Hip and knee mobility exceed the previous generation by ~40%, enabling more natural postures and reducing fatigue during extended field operations.

Helmet & Communications

An integrated HD camera in the helmet front records EVA operations for science and mission documentation. Integrated LED lights illuminate dark craters and permanently shadowed regions. Onboard 4G/LTE communications provide higher bandwidth and lower latency than past suits, enabling real-time science discussions between astronauts and ground teams. Helmet visor offers improved field of view and scratch-resistant optics.

Thermal Control

The lunar surface reaches 250°F in sunlight and -280°F in shadow. AxEMU employs advanced multi-layer insulation, liquid cooling garments, and radiator panels to maintain a comfortable 68-72°F internal environment. The suit can sustain an astronaut in permanently shadowed polar regions for up to 2 hours — critical for exploring the Moon's South Pole.

Power & Duration

Battery packs provide up to 8 hours of EVA autonomy — double the previous generation. Primary batteries support 4+ hours of active exploration; secondary battery pack provides margin. Fast-recharge capability enables rapid turnaround between EVAs during extended missions.

ARTEMIS III TESTING

Artemis III is a demonstration mission in Earth orbit, offering the first opportunity to test AxEMU in a full crewed mission context. Astronauts will don the suit during rendezvous and docking operations, validating communications, mobility, and systems integration with Orion and the HLS vehicles. No lunar EVA is planned for Artemis III, but the suit's performance in the microgravity and thermal environment of LEO will inform final readiness for Artemis IV lunar operations.

DEVELOPMENT TIMELINE

November 2025

First uncrewed thermal vacuum testing of a prototype AxEMU at Axiom's facilities in Texas. Tests validated thermal control, pressure vessel integrity, and life support component performance under simulated lunar conditions.

2026

Assembly of the first flight unit. Crewed testing at neutral buoyancy facility. Integration testing with HLS vehicles and Orion.

Mid-2027

Artemis III mission uses AxEMU for in-orbit evaluation and crew training validation.
