The Artemis III crew has not been announced as of April 2026. NASA has stated that crew announcements will come "closer to the 2027 launch window." The mission calls for four astronauts total: a commander, pilot, and two mission specialists. When crew members are announced, their names, backgrounds, and roles will be listed here.

CREW SELECTION PROCESS

Astronaut selection for Artemis missions follows a rigorous evaluation process. Candidates are drawn from the NASA astronaut corps (a mix of military test pilots, military officers, engineers, medical doctors, and scientists) and, on recent missions, from international space agencies through bilateral agreements. The commander and pilot typically have extensive spaceflight experience; mission specialists bring diverse technical expertise in geology, biology, engineering, or medicine — matching the mission's science and technical objectives.

MISSION ROLES

Commander

Leads the overall mission, serves as the primary interface with mission control, and holds authority for all crew and vehicle decisions during nominal operations and emergencies. On Artemis III, the commander will handle the primary Orion control authority during rendezvous, docking, and separation maneuvers with the Human Landing System vehicles.

Pilot

Works with the commander on spacecraft operations, assists with rendezvous and proximity operations, and monitors guidance, navigation, and control systems. Serves as a backup commander and assumes command in an emergency. On Artemis III, the pilot will perform secondary rendezvous duties and system monitoring.

Mission Specialists

Two mission specialists bring technical expertise and conduct experiments, equipment checks, and system validation. On Artemis III, mission specialists will lead HLS docking operations, AxEMU spacesuit evaluation, and rendezvous procedure validation. Their backgrounds may span engineering, geology, physics, biology, or medical science — depending on mission priorities.

TRAINING PRIORITIES FOR ARTEMIS III

Rendezvous & Docking

The commander and pilot will undergo extensive training on rendezvous navigation, proximity operations, and docking with both Starship HLS and Blue Moon Mk2 vehicles. Simulations at NASA facilities in Houston will validate procedures for nominal and contingency scenarios. The crew will train in both automated and manual control modes.

AxEMU Spacesuit Operations

All four crew members will train extensively with the Axiom AxEMU, including donning/doffing procedures, life support system operation, communications, and emergency protocols. Neutral buoyancy training in the pool will simulate mobility and task performance. Thermal vacuum chamber tests will validate the suit in simulated space conditions.

Orion Systems & Procedures

Crew will complete Orion-specific training covering launch abort systems, in-flight procedures, guidance and control, life support, emergency response, and Earth return operations. Training uses high-fidelity simulators that replicate Orion's displays, controls, and failure scenarios.

Human Landing System Integration

Mission specialists will train on HLS-specific procedures, including crew transfer, airlock operations, and contingency abort maneuvers. Contractor teams (SpaceX and Blue Origin) will provide vehicle-specific instruction. This training is critical since Artemis III is the first crewed validation of the HLS crew interfaces.

INTERNATIONAL PARTICIPATION

As on Artemis II, one or more international astronauts may be selected for Artemis III, reflecting NASA's commitment to international partnership on lunar exploration. Previous Artemis missions have included astronauts from Canada (Jeremy Hansen on Artemis II). Details on international crew participation will be announced with the full crew selection.
