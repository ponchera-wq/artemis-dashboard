Space Launch System Block 1 is the launch vehicle for Artemis III, unchanged from Artemis II. NASA cancelled plans to upgrade to Block 1B or Block 2 in February 2026 to reduce schedule risk. The Block 1 configuration uses two 5-segment solid rocket boosters, a core stage with four RS-25 engines, and an Interim Cryogenic Propulsion Stage (ICPS) upper stage. The Artemis III core stage is in processing at Kennedy Space Center.

VEHICLE CONFIGURATION

Core Stage

The rocket's backbone: a 27.6 ft diameter, 212 ft tall aluminum-lithium tank assembly holding liquid hydrogen (LH2) fuel and liquid oxygen (LOX) oxidizer. Four RS-25 engines (each generating 418,000 lbf at sea level) power the core stage from pad to approximately 70 km altitude. These engines are refurbished Space Shuttle Main Engines — heritage hardware with extensive flight history since the 1980s.

Solid Rocket Boosters (SRBs)

Two 5-segment solid boosters, each 149.16 ft tall and 12.17 ft in diameter, strap to the core stage sides. Each generates approximately 3.6 million lbf thrust at sea level. The 5-segment design (longer than Space Shuttle's 4-segment boosters) produces 40% more thrust to lift the heavier SLS payload. SRBs ignite at T-0 and burn for ~125 seconds, separating from the core stage over the Atlantic Ocean.

RS-25 Engines

The only engines powerful enough and reliable enough for SLS core stage duty. Each RS-25 has flown on the Space Shuttle (some 12-15 times) and has been recertified for Artemis. They achieve a mixture ratio of 6:1 (LOX to LH2), producing an exhaust temperature of 3,822 K. Engine nozzles can gimbal ±8° for thrust vector control.

UPPER STAGE

Interim Cryogenic Propulsion Stage (ICPS)

The ICPS is a single-use upper stage based on the Delta IV Heavy's cryogenic upper stage, modified for SLS duty. Propelled by one RL10B-2 engine (24,750 lbf), it carries SLS from Earth orbit to a trans-lunar trajectory on Artemis II and similar burn requirements on Artemis III. The ICPS measures 16.7 ft in diameter and holds LH2 fuel and LOX oxidizer. After Orion separates, the ICPS becomes an inert target for proximity operations training. The stage remains in orbit for approximately one year before re-entering.

DEVELOPMENT STATUS

The Artemis III core stage is currently in processing at Kennedy Space Center Vehicle Assembly Building. No further SLS upgrades are planned for the Artemis baseline missions following the February 2026 program restructuring. Block 1 is optimized for the renewed focus on operational flexibility and schedule confidence.
